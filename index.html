ATS Optimization Tips

Learn how to optimize your resume to pass through Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) and increase your chances of landing an interview.

Formatting Tips

  • Use standard section headings (e.g., "Work Experience", "Education")
  • Avoid tables, text boxes, headers, footers, and columns
  • Use standard fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman
  • Save your resume as a .docx or .pdf file
  • Keep formatting simple with minimal styling
  • Use standard bullet points (•) rather than custom symbols

Keyword Optimization

  • Include relevant keywords from the job description
  • Use both acronyms and spelled-out terms (e.g., "ATS" and "Applicant Tracking System")
  • Place important keywords in context, not just in a list
  • Include industry-specific terminology
  • Match the exact phrasing used in the job description
  • Avoid keyword stuffing which can trigger spam filters

Content Structure

  • Use reverse chronological order for work experience
  • Quantify achievements with numbers and percentages
  • Use action verbs to start bullet points
  • Keep bullet points concise (1-2 lines each)
  • Include dates for all experiences and education
  • Focus on relevant experience and skills for the position

ATS Compliance

  • Avoid images, graphics, and icons
  • Don't use creative section headings
  • Avoid special characters and accented letters
  • Don't include information in the header or footer
  • Use a single-column layout for better parsing
  • Test your resume with an ATS simulator before applying

Skills Optimization

  • Include a dedicated skills section
  • Categorize skills by type (technical, soft, etc.)
  • Match skills mentioned in the job description
  • Include proficiency levels for technical skills
  • Incorporate skills naturally throughout your experience
  • Remove outdated or irrelevant skills

Customization

  • Tailor your resume for each job application
  • Adjust your professional summary for each role
  • Highlight relevant experience based on the job description
  • Rearrange sections to prioritize relevant information
  • Use the same job title terminology as the listing
  • Include company-specific keywords when applicable
